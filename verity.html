AI Synthesis · Claude
Quality-weighted · 15 studies
The preponderance of peer-reviewed research published between 2020–2025 indicates that well-planned vegan diets are associated with measurable metabolic benefits, including reduced risks of cardiovascular disease, type 2 diabetes, and all-cause mortality. High-quality meta-analyses (largest dots in the map) consistently show lower BMI, LDL cholesterol, and blood pressure in plant-based cohorts — these carry the most weight in the consensus score.

A significant body of literature — particularly RCTs on muscle metabolism and cohort studies in older populations — raises concerns about nutritional adequacy without supplementation: Vitamin B12, omega-3 fatty acids (DHA/EPA), zinc, and complete protein. These studies are smaller and have lower quality scores, hence smaller dots.

The media divergence signal is notable: news coverage leans ~31% further toward "beneficial" than the scientific evidence distribution supports — with Vox, CNN Health, and Men's Health significantly over-representing positive studies while Daily Mail and New York Post anchor the concern end.
