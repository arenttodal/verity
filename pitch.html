Executive Summary

Verity answers a question nobody else is properly answering: what does the accumulated peer-reviewed evidence actually show, and how far has media coverage drifted from it? The consensus score comes from a deterministic algorithm, not an AI opinion. The media divergence layer is something no existing tool provides. The product works today.

01 — The Opportunity

The gap between what research shows
and what reaches people.

Over 4 million scientific papers are published every year. A fraction gets covered. A fraction of that coverage is accurate. And a fraction of that accuracy survives long enough to shift what people actually believe. The problem isn't that the research doesn't exist.

The Access Failure
Most high-quality research sits behind paywalls, written for specialists. The evidence exists in abundance. It just has no practical path to the people who need it.
The Translation Failure
Journalism rewards novelty and drama. A surprising new finding gets coverage; the 40 studies quietly confirming established consensus don't. The bias has a consistent direction and it compounds over years.
The Synthesis Failure
Nothing currently shows you both where the quality-weighted scientific evidence lands and how far media coverage has moved from it. That's the specific gap Verity fills.

The divergence between scientific consensus and media narrative has a direction, a magnitude, and it shapes what millions of people believe. Verity makes it visible.

02 — The Product

What a search returns.

Type any health or science question in plain language. In 20 to 30 seconds, Verity returns six things that no existing tool combines.

01
Quality-weighted consensus score
A spectrum score from Concern to Benefit, computed by a deterministic formula, not an AI opinion. Each paper is weighted by study design, bias, precision, relevance and independence.
02
Certainty level
High, Moderate, Low or Very Low, with a plain-language explanation of what's driving it. Direction and confidence are always shown separately — a topic can have a strong signal in one direction while resting on observational evidence only.
03
AI-synthesised summary
Plain-language prose written by Claude after the scoring is done. Every number in the summary comes from the computed output. Claude cannot invent a finding that wasn't in the papers.
04
Evidence strip
Every study rendered as a mark on the spectrum. Meta-analyses get wider capsule shapes; RCTs and cohort studies get thinner lines. Height reflects quality weight. The shape of the distribution often tells you more than the headline score.
05
Media divergence layer
Five years of coverage from major outlets on the same topic, scored and placed on the same spectrum as the science. The gap between where research lands and where journalism sits becomes something you can see.
06
Source sidebar
Click any mark on the evidence strip. See the abstract, quality score, framing analysis and related articles. Every number links to a real paper. The scores are computed before Claude touches the data, so there's nothing to hallucinate.
The Scoring Formula
w = Design Prior × Bias Weight × Precision × Relevance × Independence
c = Direction × Magnitude × w
score = 100 × Σ(c) / Σ|w × Magnitude|, bounded to [-100, +100]

Design priors run from 0.95 for umbrella meta-analyses down to 0.35 for cross-sectional studies. The output is a number you can interrogate, not a black box.

07 — Risks and Honest Limitations

The honest risks.

Scoring credibility
The algorithm is principled but not yet externally validated. Someone who wants to challenge the percentages will find room to. The honest answer is to recruit two or three credentialed researchers to the advisory board early, publish the methodology, and make the certainty levels prominent enough that no result looks more authoritative than it is.
Consensus adds a media layer
Consensus.app has 8 million users and could add a media layer. The window to establish the category before that happens is real but not indefinite. Press coverage of the divergence angle makes it harder to follow without looking like exactly that.
LLM commodity risk
General AI search will keep improving at citing research. The defensible position isn't summarising papers; it's the scoring algorithm, the divergence layer, and the growing cache of curated, verified results that gets harder to replicate over time.
API dependency
The product depends on three external APIs. PubMed is US government infrastructure; OpenAlex is a non-profit with strong open-access commitments; Semantic Scholar is run by the Allen Institute. Three sources in parallel means no single one is critical.
