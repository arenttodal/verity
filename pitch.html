01 — The Opportunity

The gap between science
and what people believe.

4 million papers are published every year. The gap between what research shows and what the public — and policymakers — believe is growing faster than research output itself. This is not an information scarcity problem. It is an information architecture problem.

The Access Failure
Most high-quality research sits behind paywalls, written in language inaccessible to non-specialists. The evidence exists. Nobody can reach it.
The Translation Failure
Media over-weights novel, dramatic, and commercially interesting findings. The systematic bias is not random — it is measurable and structured.
The Synthesis Failure
No tool exists that shows both where the science stands and how far media has drifted from it — simultaneously. That gap is Verity.

The divergence between scientific consensus and media narrative is not random noise — it is a measurable, structured signal. Verity makes that signal visible.

02 — The Product

What a search returns.

A user types any health or science question in natural language. Within 20–30 seconds, Verity returns six distinct outputs — each one something no existing tool produces in combination.

01
Quality-weighted consensus score
A bipolar spectrum score (Concern ↔ Benefit) derived from a deterministic algorithm — not an AI opinion. Each paper is weighted by study design, bias, precision, relevance, and independence.
02
Certainty level
High / Moderate / Low / Very Low — with a plain-language explanation of why. Direction is always separated from confidence. A topic can lean positive with very low certainty.
03
AI-synthesised summary
Plain-language prose written by Claude after the deterministic scoring is complete. The summary cannot invent numbers — every figure comes from the scored output.
04
Evidence strip
Every study as a precision mark on a spectrum. Capsule shapes for meta-analyses, thin ticks for RCTs, cohort and observational studies. Height encodes quality weight. The distribution tells you more than the number.
05
Media divergence layer
The killer feature. Guardian, BBC, NYT, Reuters — articles over 5 years, filtered to the same topic, framed by Claude. The gap between science and headlines, quantified and visualised.
06
Source sidebar
Click any tick on the strip. See the abstract, quality score, framing analysis, and related articles. Every number is traceable to a real paper. No hallucinations possible — the scores are computed before Claude writes a word.
The Scoring Formula
w = Design Prior × Bias Weight × Precision Weight × Relevance Weight × Independence Weight
c = Direction × Magnitude × w
score = 100 × Σ(c) / Σ|w × Magnitude| → bounded [-100, +100]

The AI search disruption window
Perplexity, ChatGPT, and Gemini have created a 24-month window where consumers are actively re-evaluating where they get information. Vertical, domain-specific tools that are more trustworthy than general AI have a rare moment of receptivity. This window is open now — it will narrow as habits re-solidify around 2–3 dominant platforms.

The trust collapse in health media
COVID permanently damaged credibility of both scientific institutions and mainstream health journalism. The public is simultaneously more sceptical of headlines AND more interested in understanding what research actually shows. Verity is positioned directly at that intersection.

PubMed, Semantic Scholar, and OpenAlex all opened free, high-quality APIs in the 2020–2023 period. Two years ago, building what Verity does would have required expensive data partnerships. The infrastructure cost is now near zero.

07 — Risks and Honest Limitations

What could
go wrong.

Scoring credibility
The algorithm is principled but not yet peer-reviewed. A motivated critic could challenge the percentages. Mitigation: advisory board in Month 1, methodology white paper, and prominent certainty levels that acknowledge limitations openly.
Consensus adds a media layer
The most plausible competitive threat. They have the distribution and index to do this. Mitigation: move fast on press — once the media divergence angle has coverage, it becomes harder to replicate without being seen as a follower.
LLM commodity risk
General AI search will get better at citing papers. Mitigation: the moat is not "AI can summarise papers" — it is the deterministic scoring algorithm, the media divergence layer, and the proprietary query cache accumulating over time.
API dependency
The product depends on Semantic Scholar, PubMed, and OpenAlex APIs. PubMed is US government infrastructure. Mitigation: three sources in parallel with graceful fallback — no single source is critical.
